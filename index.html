1. Problem & Outcome

Any initiative must have a positive and measurable business impact.

Connecting Learning to Business Value

There must be a clear flow from strategic goals to learning programs. We start with the business challenge, not the learning solution.

  • 1Cost of Living Crisis: To remain a value leader, our operations must be intentional and hyper-efficient.
  • 2Intense Competition: We can't be trend-followers; we must be trend-setters, requiring constant innovation and speed.
  • 3Dependence on Brick-and-Mortar: The in-store experience is everything. Our colleagues are our most critical brand ambassadors.

2. Evaluation

The only metrics that matter are those that shift business performance.

Business Results are our Learning Metrics

After three retrenchments in L&D, the days of relying on "learning metrics" like course satisfaction are over. The business can't and won't rely on theory; it needs tangible results. It is imperative that we L&D practitioners take accountability. The only metrics that matter are those that shift business performance, such as improved customer satisfaction, increased sales, or reduced process times—delivering actual, measurable results.

  • ✓Define Business KPIs First: Partner with stakeholders to identify the core business metric we intend to move.
  • ✓Connect Performance to Learning: We need to instill a culture of learning and reward those that seek out to improve.
  • ✓Intentional Learning Design: Create to enable impact.

3. Target Audience

To be impactful at a global scale, training must be intentionally interconnected.

Guiding Philosophy

To be impactful at a global scale, training must be intentionally interconnected. A change in behaviour on the shop floor can only be sustained if it is understood, reinforced, and supported by leadership and central teams. This is a manufactured outcome, as it is part of the set objectives for the initiative.

An 8-month development cycle for 20 people? As a global operations function, we no longer have the luxury of creating training that doesn't reach a global audience and isn't interconnected and impactful at all levels. This means the training must be intentionally designed to support and enable this interconnectedness.

Our Audience Tiers

  • Train the Trainers

    Who: Store Leadership (Team Leads, Store Managers) who will be equipped to deliver the training.

    Learning Needs: They need to master the core content, but more importantly, they need to be equipped with the facilitation and coaching skills to effectively train their teams, reinforce the desired behaviours, and make the learning "stick."

  • Direct Intervention

    Who: The customer-facing retail colleagues on the shop floor.

    Learning Needs: Practical, simple, and immediately applicable skills that help them perform their jobs more effectively and confidently. Training must be concise, fit into the flow of their work, and directly address the real-world challenges they face.

  • Awareness

    Who: Central Functions (e.g., Operations, HR, Merchandising) that support the retail network.

    Learning Needs: They don't need the "how-to" training, but they need high-level awareness of the initiatives being rolled out in stores. This ensures they can provide the right support, align their own processes, and understand how their decisions impact the in-store experience.

4. Agile Design

Learning should be iterative, data-driven, and built for rapid deployment.

From MVP to Scaled Solution

We don't spend months building the "perfect" solution. We launch a Minimum Viable Product (MVP), gather real-world data, and iterate quickly.

  • 1Prototype & Test: Quickly build and test low-fidelity prototypes with the target audience to validate concepts.
  • 2Launch an MVP: Release a core version of the training to a pilot group to measure initial impact and gather feedback.
  • 3Iterate Based on Data: Use performance metrics and qualitative feedback to refine and enhance the learning experience.

5. Formats & Methods

The question isn't what format we use, but what's the fastest path to the business result.

Guiding Principle

The question is not whether we make an eLearning module or a video. The question is: what is the fastest, most effective way to achieve the desired business result? If a concept can be taught in a 30-second TikTok, that's what we'll use. Our methods must be simple, scalable, and designed for a fast-paced retail environment, minimizing disruption while maximizing impact.

Our Approach Will Be:

  • Just-in-Time

    Learning should be available at the moment of need, moving away from scheduled courses and towards on-demand resources.

  • Multi-Modal

    Using a mix of formats (video, text, audio, interactive) to cater to different learning preferences.

  • Multi-Language

    All content will be created for global scalability, ensuring it can be easily translated and localized.

  • Usable & Reusable

    Information locked in a one-off eLearning course is not effective. We will create shareable, reusable knowledge assets.

  • Social & Transformable

    Our formats will encourage peer-to-peer sharing and allow information to be transformed and adapted for different contexts.

6. Engagement & Comms

Great training is useless if no one knows it exists or why it matters.

Creating a Pull for Learning

Let's be honest—people don't care about training unless it directly benefits them and their careers. To be engaging, our initiatives must clearly and compellingly answer the "What's in it for me?.

  • ✓Leadership Buy-in: Engage leaders to champion the initiative and reinforce its importance.
  • ✓Targeted Communication: Develop a multi-channel comms plan to build awareness and excitement.
  • ✓Feedback Loops: Create clear channels for learners to share feedback, feel heard, and see their input acted upon.

7. Stakeholder Engagement

We move from "getting buy-in" to a model of instant, collaborative knowledge generation.

Guiding Philosophy: Real-Time Partnership

The traditional method of getting sign-off is antiquated and too slow. We must move from "getting buy-in" to a model where knowledge is generated instantly and collaboratively. This means building active partnerships with stakeholders and creating feedback loops that allow us to listen, co-create, and adapt in real-time.

Live Example: Real-Time Engagement Loop

Let's use an extreme but powerful case. Imagine a security incident occurs in one of our stores.

Immediate Feedback

Colleagues start posting on internal channels about how they feel—scared, angry, and confused. These are instant, real-time data on employee sentiment and data to show an immediate need for intervention.

Global Ops Response

Instead of a delayed, formal communication, Global Ops responds immediately. They issue a mandate to Team Leads to check in with their teams.

Leadership in Action

Senior leaders start publicly praising the great leadership shown by retail staff on the ground. They highlight immediate, positive actions being taken.

What this demonstrates:

This real-time loop shows that we are listening and reacting instantly. It builds trust and shows that central functions are connected to the reality of the stores. This is the level of engagement we should aim for with our learning initiatives—a constant, responsive dialogue, not a one-way broadcast.

8. Scaling

All programs are designed to scale globally from day one.

Guiding Philosophy

All programs are designed to scale globally from day one. As Global Ops, we handle global challenges. More bespoke, personalized training initiatives should be handled by internal and local teams, not by our function.

To achieve this, we will:

  • Create a Global Product with a Localization Kit

    Design a single, high-quality "80%" solution that is universally applicable, and provide regional teams with a simple toolkit to adapt the remaining "20%" for local context.

  • Utilize a Train-the-Trainer Model

    Empower local leaders to be the delivery agents. This is the most efficient and effective way to scale facilitation and ensure local relevance.

  • Leverage Technology

    Leverage a centralized platform to distribute the core product and localization kits, streamlining workflows and enhancing access for all markets, moving beyond a traditional LMS-only model.

  • Foster Cross-Functional Partnerships

    Our initiatives will not operate in isolation. We will work closely with Leadership, Department Leads, Marketing, and other key functions to ensure alignment, gather support, and guarantee a successful global rollout.
